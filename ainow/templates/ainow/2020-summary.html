TICTeC 2020

24 & 25 March 2020, Reykjavik, Iceland

  • Register

Returning for a sixth year, TICTeC 2020 will focus on the impact that civic technology and digital democracy are having upon citizens, decision makers and governments around the world.

Primarily, the goal of TICTeC is to promote and share rigorous and meaningful research into online civic technologies and digital democracy around the world. The conference facilitates discussion and networking amongst individuals and groups to find real-world solutions through sharing evidence of impact, and (importantly) evidence of what doesn’t work.

Hosted by UK-based NGO mySociety, TICTeC 2020 will bring together individuals from academic and applied backgrounds as well as businesses, public authorities, NGOs, funders and education institutions to discuss ideas, present research and build a network of individuals interested in the civic technology landscape. See who’s previously attended TICTeC or download our guide to TICTeC for more information.

Invitation letters & visas

Conference policy

If you need a travel visa to Iceland, we will be glad to issue an invitation letter to support your visa application. If you require a visa, it is recommended you apply for one at least six weeks before you travel. Invitation letters will only be issued once your registration and payment have been completed. Invitation letters will be sent by e-mail. In order to request an invitation letter, please e-mail us with the following data:

  • a good-quality scan of your passport (.pdf or image format)
  • surname/family name and given names, exactly as they appear in your passport
  • contacts: e-mail and phone number
  • affiliation/organisation: name, address, country, and the position you hold/occupy
  • any additional information you may think is relevant to your application

General information

Accessibility information

The conference venue is fully accessible for wheelchair users and provides a special infrared sound system for guests with hearing aids. Parking for disabled people is available next to the entry doors from the car park into the building. If you plan to attend TICTeC with a personal care attendant/assistant, their conference fee will be waived. Please contact Gemma Moulder on +44 (0)20 3239 0725 or via email to arrange the fee waiver/registration.

If you need special requirements such as hearing loops, sign language interpreters, handouts in advance and/or in alternative formats such as Braille or large print etc, please contact Gemma Moulder on +44 (0)20 3239 0725 or via email with your request by close of business on 24th February 2020 so that arrangements, where possible, can be made. We will do our best to fulfil your requirements to allow you to fully participate in this event.

Language

The conference language is English. Sign language interpretation is available upon request (please submit by 24th February 2020 through the registration form).

Code of conduct

All attendees, speakers and sponsors at TICTeC conferences are required to agree with our code of conduct, which sets out our anti-harassment policy and tells you where you can report violations. Organisers will enforce this code throughout the event. We expect cooperation from all participants to help ensure a safe environment for everybody.
