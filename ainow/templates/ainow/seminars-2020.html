TICTeC Seminars 2020

Online, September–November 2020

If more digitalisation, transparency and accountability mechanisms had been adopted before the coronavirus pandemic hit, many outcomes would have been better or different today. Civic tech, digital democracy and open government organisations have been championing these reforms and mechanisms for decades, with the pandemic serving as a tragic example of why these are essential and not just ‘nice to have’.

Join mySociety and guests this autumn for a series of discussions to reflect on the impacts of this, and why digital transparency and accountability is more relevant than ever going forward.

Discussion 1 Open data: Essential, not just ‘nice to have’

22 September 2020, 15:00 GMT+1 to 16:00 GMT+1

  • Register here

In this discussion we’ll focus on how having more open data, and access to data, would have helped with responding to the COVID-19 crisis. We’ll focus particularly on how more open contracting and beneficial ownership data would have helped governments the world over to make quicker and safer purchases of essential equipment to tackle COVID-19.

Discussion 2 Parliaments: The power of digitising

20 October 2020, 15:00 GMT+1 to 16:00 GMT+1

  • Register here

Things that once were deemed by governments as impossible or not important enough - like voting remotely - have become a reality in some parliaments across the world since the emergence of COVID-19. This session will get to the crux of why it’s taken a global pandemic to get more parliaments to digitise and how new measures should be harnessed so future opportunities are not missed.

Discussion 3 Civic tech’s role in tackling the climate crisis

15 November 2020, 15:00 GMT+1 to 16:00 GMT+1

  • Register here

The climate emergency is a parallel crisis we need to tackle. With COP26 delayed until 2021, this discussion will focus on how the Civic Tech community can use this time to clarify its role in tackling the climate crisis. Is the Civic Tech community doing enough? Should civic tech shift its focus? What can be learnt from the COVID-19 pandemic to ensure better decisions are taken and opportunities are not missed?
